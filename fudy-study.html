Fudy Order & Pay

I joined the company in the middle of 2023 as a Lead Product Designer.

At the time Fudy was about to transition from a food delivery service to a hospitality sales and marketing platform. Within 10 months we created a QR-ordering product for restaurants, bars and other venues. With Order & Pay, we focused on creating an immediate, personal in-person dining experience.

I built the foundation for a component library with design tokens, making it easier to create a flexible white-label product. I designed web apps for both customers and kitchens. I also created unique QR code sticker templates in Figma.

In this case study I'll discuss how and why I created a design system. I will explain the reason for this project, the problems before and future, the design principles, methodologies and tools I used.

Order & Pay

Problem

Like many early-stage startups prioritizing speed over structure, Fudy's existing component library was hastily created during rapid product development cycles. This approach created significant design inconsistencies across the product ecosystem.

The challenge was further complicated by maintaining separate codebases for native iOS, Android, and Web applications. Component library, or a design system, should serve as a single source of truth, that makes product development more efficient. Instead, it slowed us down.

Decison

We quickly realized, that it would be more difficult and time-consuming to try and salvage the existing component library, rather than build a new system.

Primary catalyst for this iniative was the launch of Figma's Variables. This feature unlocked new opportunities for us, providing a native, scalable way to manage the design tokens essential for our white-label architecture. We also saw it as the key to bring design and code closer to each other.

Design tokens and variables

Figma Variables are the representation of design tokens. Naturally, we wanted to achieve a common shared language across our codebase.

Previous Figma-facing design system used Styles to define color, typography and styles, which don't translate well into code. First thing was to migrate all Styles to Variables, except for gradients, which are not yet supported (at the time of writing this case study in 2024).

Variables allowed us to create the multi-layered token structure that was essential for our white-label platform. We created our system to separate primitive tokens (raw non-contextual values like blue-500 or 16px) from semantic tokens (specific purpose, like background-brand or container-padding).

Success criteria

  • Create an intuitive, reliable in-venue ordering experience.
  • Build a flexible, white-label product adaptable for multiple venues through Figma variables.
  • Develop a design system foundation to ensure consistency across apps and marketing.

Deliverables

  • Web applications for customers, kitchens and admin.
  • Design System and component library built using Figma.
  • Marketing design components, including templates and QR code sticker layouts.

Highlights

  • Led the design of both customer-facing and kitchen web apps, ensuring clarity and efficiency on both ends.
  • Established the foundation for a scalable component library using Figma’s Variables, improving cross-platform consistency.
  • Applied Atomic and Modular Design principles to improve reusability and flexibility of components.

Constraints

  • Legacy inconsistencies in the previous design system caused initial delays.
  • Balancing product speed (innovation) with system stability (design governance).

Outcome

  • Delivered a clean, semantic design system that reduced design debt and increased alignment between design and development.
  • Enabled faster iteration cycles for new restaurant partners through reusable, themeable design tokens.
